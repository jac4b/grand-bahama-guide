üìç Port Lucaya - Boutique dive operation. Offers wreck penetration, cave diving, and advanced certifications. Small groups only.

Popular Dive Sites

Shark Junction

45-50 feet deep - Famous shark feeding dive. Encounter Caribbean reef sharks in their natural habitat. Advanced Open Water required.

Theo's Wreck

90-100 feet deep - 230-foot freighter sunk in 1982. Covered in corals and sponges. Perfect for underwater photography. Advanced divers.

Ben's Cavern

20-40 feet deep - Underwater cave system with stunning stalactites and stalagmites. Good visibility. Cavern certification recommended.

Pygmy Caves

40-60 feet deep - Beautiful reef with swim-throughs and abundant marine life. Sea turtles, groupers, and angelfish. Great for all levels.

ü§ø Diving Tips & Information

  • Certification: Bring your PADI/SSI certification card and dive log
  • Best Diving Season: Year-round, but April-November offers warmest water (80-84¬∞F)
  • Visibility: Typically 60-100+ feet, some sites reach 150+ feet
  • Book in Advance: Popular dives (especially shark dives) fill up quickly
  • Two-Tank Dives: Most operators offer morning two-tank trips ($120-150)
  • Equipment: Full rental packages available ($40-60/day)
  • Discover Scuba: Never dived? Try an intro dive ($99-150)
  • Medical: No dive medical required for recreational diving if healthy
  • Flying: Wait 18-24 hours after diving before your return ferry/flight

üèñÔ∏è Beach & Water Activities

Pristine Beaches

Visit Taino Beach and Gold Rock Beach for stunning turquoise waters and powdery white sand. Gold Rock Beach is often ranked among the most beautiful beaches in the world.

Snorkeling Adventures

Explore Peterson Cay National Park and Deadman's Reef. Crystal-clear waters teeming with colorful fish, sea turtles, and vibrant coral formations.

Discover underwater caves, dramatic walls, and historic shipwrecks. Perfect for both beginners and experienced divers with sites ranging from 15-100+ feet.

Swim with dolphins at Sanctuary Bay for an unforgettable experience. Interact with these intelligent creatures in their natural habitat.

Kayaking

Paddle through Lucayan National Park's serene mangrove creeks. Spot tropical birds, baby fish, and enjoy the peaceful natural surroundings.

üå¥ Land Activities

Experience local culture with shopping, waterfront dining, live music, and authentic Bahamian crafts. Perfect for evening strolls and entertainment.

Garden of the Groves

Wander through 12 acres of botanical gardens featuring waterfalls, flowering plants, and a chapel. Home to over 10,000 plant species and native wildlife.

‚öì Booking & Planning Tips

  • Book Early: Popular charters (especially fishing & sunset cruises) book 2-3 weeks in advance during peak season
  • Weather Dependent: All charters are weather-dependent. Most operators offer full refunds for weather cancellations
  • Gratuity: 15-20% tip is customary for captains and crew, usually not included in price
  • What to Bring: Sunscreen (reef-safe), sunglasses, hat, towel, camera, and motion sickness medication if needed
  • Private vs Shared: Private charters cost more but offer flexibility in schedule and destinations
  • Food & Drinks: Some charters include lunch/snacks, others allow you to bring your own (BYOB often welcomed)
  • Group Discounts: Many operators offer discounts for groups of 6+
  • Best Times: Morning departures (8-9am) often have calmer seas; sunset cruises (5-7pm) offer stunning views
